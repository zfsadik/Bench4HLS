A linear feedback shift register is a shift register usually with a few XOR gates to produce the next state of the shift register. A Galois LFSR is one particular arrangement that shifts right, where a bit position with a "tap" is XORed with the LSB output bit (q[0]) to produce its next value, while bit positions without a tap shift right unchanged.  Build a 32-bit Galois LFSR with taps at bit positions 32, 22, 2, and 1. Reset should be active high synchronous, and should reset the output q to 32'h1.

The top-level function should have the following prototype:
void TopModule(bool reset, ap_uint<32> &q)
