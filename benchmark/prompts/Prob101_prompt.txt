Build a 100-bit left/right rotator, with synchronous load and left/right enable. A rotator shifts-in the shifted-out bit from the other end of the register, unlike a shifter that discards the shifted-out bit and shifts in a zero. If enabled, a rotator rotates the bits around and does not modify/discard them.

  (1) load: Loads shift register with data[100] instead of rotating.
      Synchronous active high.

  (2) ena[2]: Synchronous. Chooses whether and which direction to
      rotate:
      (a) 2'b01 rotates right by one bit,
      (b) 2'b10 rotates left by one bit,
      (c) 2'b00 and 2'b11 do not rotate.

  (3) q: The contents of the rotator (Output).

The top-level function should have the following prototype:
void TopModule(bool load, ap_uint<2> ena, ap_uint<100> data, ap_uint<100> &q)
